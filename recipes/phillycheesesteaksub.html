Philly Cheesesteak Sub

Description

A Philly Cheesesteak is a sandwich made from slices of beefsteak and melted cheese in a long hoagie roll. It's a popular regional food in the Northeast.

The Philly Cheesesteak has its origins in Philadelphia where hotdog stand owners decided to make a sandwich from grilled onions and chopped beef for their own lunch. While they were eating, a taxi driver stopped by and requested one for himself and recommended they start selling this sandwhich with cheese on it.

Ingredients

  • 2 eight inch hoagie rolls sliced
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/2 green bell pepper sliced
  • 1/2 onion sliced
  • 4 button mushrooms sliced
  • 16 oz. boneless ribeye steak thin sliced
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 teaspoons worcestershire sauce
  • 4 slices of white american cheese

Directions

  1. Butter inside of rolls with 1 teaspoon of butter and toast the bread
  2. On medium heat, add 1 tablespoon of butter. Once melted and bubbling, add the bell peppers, onions, and mushrooms.
  3. Mix into pan and stir occasionally until vegetables are lightly browned and onions turn transparent
  4. Remove from pan and set aside
  5. Season the thinly sliced ribeye steak with salt and pepper.
  6. Add 1 tablespoon of butter to pan, and once it's melted and bubbling, add the ribeye and cook for 2-4 minutes, stirring occasionally
  7. Add worchestershire sauce to skillet and stir to mix
  8. Reduce heat to low, return the vegetables to the pan and stir.
  9. Split the mixture in half, lay two pieces of cheese onto each half and allow it to melt for about a minute
  10. Spoon the mixture into the hoagie and serve immediately
