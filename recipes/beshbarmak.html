Beshbarmak

Description

Beshbarmak is a Central Asian dish originating from Kazakhstan, Uzbeikstan, and Kyrgyzstan. It's made from chopped meat, onions and mixed with egg noodle dough. A chicken, beef, or horse meat broth is served to drink with the food.

Beshbarmak literally means "five-finger" because the food is put into a huge serving bowl in the middle of the table and everyone can grab what however much they desire. Before the development of plates and silverware, the food was eaten with a person's hands with everyone grabbing the food from the plate and taking however much they desire.

Ingredients

  • Chicken, Whole
  • 1 Onion, Whole
  • 1 Full Carrot
  • Salt
  • 1 Bay Leaf
  • Dough
    • Flour, 650 grams
    • Water - 350 ml
    • Eggs, 2
    • Salt, 1 pinch
  • Filling
    • Onions 3 or 4
    • Ground Black Pepper

Steps

  1. Peel and was onions and carrots
  2. Put chicken, carrots, onion and bay leaf into a pot of water
  3. Boil the chicken over a low heat without covering the pan wiht a lid so the broth remains transparent
  4. After boiling, remove the foam with a sieve
  5. While the chicken is boiling, start making the dough
    1. First, disolve salt into water
    2. Crack the eggs into a bowl of flour and pour the water slowly into the bowl
    3. Knead the boudh until it is firm and elastic
    4. Roll the dough into a thin layer on a flour covered table
    5. Cut the dough into rectanges and let them dry
  6. Leave the chicken to finish boiling in the broth
  7. Then, take out the chicken, vegetables, and bay leaf from the broth
  8. Salt hte broth and bring to a boil
  9. Cut the onions into thin half rings
  10. Pour the onions into the boiling broth and add black ground pepper
  11. Leave the onion in the broth for 5-7 minutes then put it on a sieve and let the liquid drain
  12. Separate the chicken meat from the bones and cut into medium pieces
  13. In the boiling chicken broth, but the dough rectanges until they pop up
  14. Then, arrange the rectangular pieces of dough on a plate with a little browth. Then, add the meat in a circle, put the onions in the center and sprinkle some black pepper on top
